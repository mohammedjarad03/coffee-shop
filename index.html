Types of coffee

Expresso

Espresso is a form of concentrated coffee, served in shots. It's made with two ingredients, 100% finely ground coffee and hot water. This method of preparation produces a strong, rich, and concentrated shot of coffee, which forms the basis for many other types of coffee, such as cappuccino, latte, americano, and macchiato.

Caffè Latte

A latte, or as it is known as a café latte, is a coffee with milk that originated in Italy. It is served with a soft layer of foam and this makes it similar to a cappuccino, but there are clear differences such as the amount of milk, water and coffee combined. Withstands.

Mocha Cafe

The drink is believed to have been named primarily after the mocha bean, a type of coffee bean that was shipped from the port of Mocha in Yemen. Mocha beans are known for their natural chocolate flavor. Now the name "mocha" no longer refers to this type of coffee bean, but rather to the presence of chocolate flavors added to the coffee drink.
